1. Overview
1.1 Purpose
TransitOps is a centralized, web-based transport operations platform that digitizes the end-to-end management of a logistics fleet — vehicle registration, driver management, trip dispatch, maintenance, fuel and expense tracking, and operational analytics. 
This PRD defines the goals, users, functional scope, business rules, and success criteria for the platform.
1.2 Business Context
Many logistics companies still rely on spreadsheets and manual logbooks to manage transport operations. This creates recurring 
operational problems:
Scheduling conflicts from double-booked vehicles or drivers
Underutilized vehicles due to lack of real-time visibility into fleet status
Missed or delayed maintenance leading to breakdowns and safety risk
Expired driver licenses going
 unnoticed until a compliance incident occurs
Inaccurate or fragmented expense tracking across fuel, tolls, and repairs
Poor operational visibility for leadership and finance teams
TransitOps addresses these problems with a single system of record that enf
orces business rules automatically, keeps vehicle and driver status synchronized with real-world operations, and surfaces operational and financial insight through dashboards and reports.
1.3 Goals & Objectives
Provide one authoritative source of truth for fleet, driver, trip, maintenance, and expense data.
Eliminate invalid dispatches by enforcing capacity, license, and availability rules automatically at the point of trip creation.
Give Fleet Managers real-time
 visibility into fleet utilization, vehicle status, and maintenance backlog.
Give Safety Officers proactive visibility into license expiry and safety-score trends before they become compliance incidents.
Give Financial Analysts consolidated, per-vehicle co
st and profitability data (fuel, maintenance, ROI) without manual reconciliation.
Reduce the operational overhead of running a fleet on spreadsheets, with secure, role-based access for every stakeholder.

As a Fleet Manager, I want to see a real-time dashboard of Active, Available, and In-Shop vehicles so I know current fleet capacity at a glance.
As a Fleet Manager, I want to open a maintenance record for a vehicle so it is automatically pulled out of the 
dispatch pool while being serviced.
As a Fleet Manager, I want to filter fleet data by vehicle type, status, and region so I can manage specific segments of the fleet.
As a Fleet Manager, I want to retire a vehicle so it is permanently excluded from dispat
ch and reporting reflects its full lifecycle.

As a Driver/Dispatcher, I want to create a trip by selecting a source, destination, available vehicle, and available driver so that dispatch is fast and error-free.
As a Driver/Dispatcher, I want the system to block trip creation if cargo weight exceeds the vehicle's maximum load capacity so I never overload a vehicle.
As a Driver/Dispatcher, I want vehicle and driver status to update automatically on dispatch and completion so I don't have to manually reset statuses.
As a Driver/Dispatcher, I want to cancel a dispatched trip and have the vehicle and driver restored to A
vailable so resources aren't stuck as unavailable.
As a Driver/Dispatcher, I want to view all active and pending trips in one place so I can monitor deliveries in progress.

As a Safety Officer, I want to view each driver's license catego
ry and expiry date so I can track compliance across the fleet.
As a Safety Officer, I want the system to prevent assignment of drivers with expired licenses or Suspended status so unsafe dispatches never happen.
As a Safety Officer, I want to monitor each 
driver's safety score over time so I can identify drivers who need coaching or review.
As a Safety Officer, I want to suspend a driver's status so they are immediately removed from the trip-assignment pool.
As a Safety Officer, I want (bonus) email reminde
rs before a license expires so I can act proactively instead of reactively.
3.4 Financial Analyst
As a Financial Analyst, I want to log fuel and expense records per vehicle so operational costs are captured accurately.
As a Financial Analyst, I want the sy
stem to automatically compute total operational cost (fuel + maintenance) per vehicle so I don't need manual reconciliation.
As a Financial Analyst, I want to view Fuel Efficiency, Fleet Utilization, Operational Cost, and Vehicle ROI in one report so I can
 assess profitability per asset.
As a Financial Analyst, I want to export reports to CSV so I can share and further analyze the data outside the platform.

As any user, I want to log in securely with email and password, and only see the featur

5. Functional Requirements
5.1 Authentication
Implement secure login using email and password.
Support Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) for Fleet Manager, Driver, Safety Officer, and Financial Analyst roles.
Only authenticated users may access any part of the application; unauthenticated requests redirect to login.
5.2 Dashboard
Display KPIs: Active
 Vehicles, Available Vehicles, Vehicles in Maintenance, Active Trips, Pending Trips, Drivers On Duty, and Fleet Utilization (%).
Provide filters by vehicle type, status, and region.
5.3 Vehicle Registry
Maintain a master list of vehicles with: Registration
 Number (unique), Vehicle Name/Model, Type, Maximum Load Capacity, Odometer, Acquisition Cost, and Status.
Status values: Available, On Trip, In Shop, Retired.
5.4 Driver Management
Maintain driver profiles with: Name, License Number, License Category, License Expiry Date, Contact Number, Safety Score, and Status.
Status values: Available, On Trip, Off Duty, Suspended.
5.5 Trip Management
Create trips by selecting source, destination,
 an available vehicle, an available driver, cargo weight, and planned distance.
Trip lifecycle: Draft → Dispatched → Completed → Cancelled.
5.6 Maintenance
Create maintenance records for vehicles.
Adding a vehicle to a Maintenance Log automatically switche
s its status to In Shop, removing it from the driver/dispatch selection pool.
5.7 Fuel & Expense Management
Record fuel logs (liters, cost, date) and other expenses such as tolls or maintenance.
Automatically compute total operational cost (Fuel + Maintena
nce) per vehicle.
5.8 Reports & Analytics
Display Fuel Efficiency (Distance / Fuel), Fleet Utilization, Operational Cost, and Vehicle ROI.
Vehicle ROI is calculated as:
ROI = ( Revenue − (Maintenance + Fuel) ) / Acquisition Cost
Support CSV export; PDF export is optional (nice-to-have).
6. Mandatory Business Rules
These rules are enforced by the system, not left to user discipline, since they encode the safety and integrity constraints of real-world fleet operations:
The vehicle r
egistration number must be unique.
Retired or In Shop vehicles must never appear in the dispatch selection.
Drivers with expired licenses or Suspended status cannot be assigned to trips.
A driver or vehicle already marked On Trip cannot be assigned to anot
her trip.
Cargo weight must not exceed the vehicle's maximum load capacity.
Dispatching a trip automatically changes both the vehicle and driver status to On Trip.
Completing a trip automatically changes both the vehicle and driver status back to Available.
Cancelling a dispatched trip restores the vehicle and driver to Available.
Creating an active maintenance record automatically changes vehicle status to In Shop.
Closing a maintenance record restores the vehicle to Available (unless it is Retired).

10. Assumptions & Constraints
A vehicle and a driver can each be assigned to only one active trip at a time.
Safety Score is maintained/updated outside automated trip logic in v1 (e.g., manually by the Safety Officer), unless a scoring engine is scoped separately.
Revenue per trip (used in the ROI calculation) is entered or derived from trip/billing data; the exa
ct revenue source is to be confirmed with Finance.
Regions used for dashboard filtering are configured as reference data (e.g., assigned at the vehicle or route level).
